Water

Cooling Systems

AI infrastructure generates significant heat. Data centers rely on cooling systems to maintain operational temperatures. Many cooling methods require water, particularly evaporative cooling systems.

Water may be used directly in cooling towers or indirectly through electricity generation processes that depend on steam and thermal regulation.

Regional Impact

Water consumption varies by geography. Facilities located in drought-prone or water- stressed regions can place additional pressure on local supplies.

Water Use Considerations in AI Infrastructure

Variable Influence on Water Demand Sustainability Concern
Cooling Method Determines direct water use Evaporative systems increase consumption
Climate Affects cooling load Hotter climates require more cooling
Regional Water Availability Determines resilience Water-stressed areas face greater risk
Seasonal Demand Patterns Impacts supply stability Peak periods can strain infrastructure

As AI infrastructure expands, water resource management becomes an increasingly important sustainability issue.
